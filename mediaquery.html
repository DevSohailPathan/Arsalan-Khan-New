How Computer Mice Work

A "mouse" can refer to the small rodent mammal with a pointed nose, large ears, and a long tail, or a computer mouseâ€”a hdevice invented by Douglas Engelbart to control a computer cursor and perform actions like selecting and dragging on a graphical user interface. Animal mice are nocturnal omnivores with short lifespans, while computer mice are input devices that can be wired or wireless, using either light sensors or a physical ball to track movement.

Mice first broke onto the public stage with the introduction of the Apple
Macintosh in 1984, and since then they have helped to completely redefine the way we use computers.
